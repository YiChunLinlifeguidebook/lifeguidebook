## Summary

Since 1901, the Nobel Peace Prize has been awarded **111 times** (as of 2024) to **90 individuals** and **30 organizations**. It has **not been awarded** 19 times, mostly during the World Wars. The prize reflects humanity's enduring pursuit of a world free from war, oppression, poverty, and injustice.

Notable themes across the awards include:
- **Disarmament & nuclear non-proliferation** — from the Pugwash Conferences to ICAN
- **Human rights & civil liberties** — Martin Luther King Jr., Aung San Suu Kyi, Liu Xiaobo
- **Women's rights & gender equality** — Malala Yousafzai, the 2011 trio
- **Conflict resolution & mediation** — Nelson Mandela, John Hume, Abiy Ahmed
- **Humanitarian aid** — Red Cross, UNHCR, Médecins Sans Frontières
- **Environmental protection** — Wangari Maathai, Al Gore, IPCC

Each laureate represents a beacon of hope in their time — a reminder that peace is not merely the absence of war, but the active presence of justice, dignity, and compassion in the world.
